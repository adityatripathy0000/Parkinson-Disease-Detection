About the Disease

Parkinson’s disease is a neurological disorder of the central nervous system that affects movement, often including tremors. It occurs when nerve cells in the brain stop producing the chemical dopamine. Without dopamine, nerve cells can’t properly send messages, causing the loss of muscle function which helps control muscle movement. Parkinson's often starts with a tremor in one hand. Other symptoms are slow movement, stiffness and loss of balance. It affects more than 1 million individuals every year in India. It affects a half million people in the United States, with about 50,000 newly diagnosed cases each year. There is no cure and until now, no reliable method for detecting the disease. Diagnosis is based on a person's medical history and a neurological examination.

About the Product

A detection method is a major breakthrough in diagnosing Parkinson’s in early stages, the point at which treatment to control symptoms is most effective. XGBoost is an open-source software library which provides a gradient boosting framework and it aims to provides a Scalable, Portable and Distributed Gradient Boosting that solve many data science problems in a fast and accurate way. Our Mission

The increase in rate of Parkinson’s disease is a matter of great concern and with the inadequate healthcare systems makes for a recipe of disaster. With the increase in growth of machine learning applications, which are being used in almost every working class sector, it was pretty obvious for the creation of a program that would target Parkinson’s disease.
